Get ready for Katowice
 

A little from Group A

IEM Katowice groups have been revealed, and there is plenty of mouth-watering Counter-Strike for your eyes to be treated with. Our favourite Dutchman napz (sorry, chrisj) pinned this event as having a 'banger rating' of '2015 NiKo on a deagle'. Now that is high praise.

While you'll only be able to watch half the games, unless you have two monitors and four eyes, due to ESL's decision to play two games at once, that means you can miss out the duds like... well, never mind. 

Astralis 2- sorry, Astralis vs Cloud9 is the first game, with NiP taking on ZyWoo concurrently. They're technically both best of threes, but realistically, two maps will be enough if Vitality play like they did at BLAST. And despite their poor performance at BLAST, there's just no way Astralis conspire to lose to C9? Haha jk...

Unless?

NiP magic is a real thing, and though it usually refers to them somehow beating a team they had no right to, with the way Vitality have looked recently, maybe NiP magic is somehow finding a way to lose.

zonic thinks they would have lost to anyone with the way they played at BLAST, but C9? Losing to C9 would be disaster for a slow-starting Astralis (in 2020, at least) and would surely send shockwaves through the whole of Denmark. Even Kjaerbye might be a little worried if Astralis lost. Somewhere behind that beaming smile.

NaVi take on FaZe in the 'we kicked GuardiaN' derby, and this could be the best game of them all. With s1mple and electronic's stunning performances at BLAST to take down Astralis, and FaZe looking rejuvenated, this is a thigh-rub-inducing banger match-up.

Renegades vs fnatic shouldn't take too long on paper, but Renegades (what used to be Grayhound) do love a surprise. Without DickStacy their marketability and general interest is down, but the skill level has probably gone up, and they could cause an upset.

Kato continued
 

...and a little from Group B

mouz take on TyLoo, who were a late call-up for ViCi Gaming, as one of their player's VISA applications was postponed due to concerns growing over the Coronavirus. We're not sure it matters which team turns up, as mouz in current form are as dangerous and unstoppable as the Coronavirus, but like, a computer virus.

100T take on G2, and Australia take on France in the anti-Britain derby. This one is actually quite an intriguing game, and picking a winner is harder than going from Britain to the rest of Europe nowadays. 100T are generally the better team, but they're inconsistent and G2 are a mercurial outfit too.

G2 added two new coaching members this week, and with a bit of luck, one of them might know how to plant the bomb and the other how to translate English to French for JaCKz. Swani clearly has a good sense of humour, joking (we assume) that he wants to 'pick up where they left off' in 'winning trophies'. He might have the wrong esport.

TL take on VP at midday the next day, and with rumours of n0rb3r7 joining are true, the CIS team is likely in some sort of turmoil. If we've learnt anything about VP, though, it's that that probably means they're going to win.

MAD Lions and EG round out the groups, with EG probably favourites against the plucky underdogs. MAD Lions have been going crazy recently, but EG is likely a step too far.

Semmler doesn't want esports to continue the way it is now

Illustration by Crash_. Photo from Semmler

We were always allied with GGRecon!

Don't worry - this is the same company we've always been working with. They've just been forced to retcon from GGIntel to GGRecon after some unnamed producer of high quality chips weren't happy. Our money is on McCains.

One person who doesn't care for chips is Semmler - because he wants 'no fat on this beast'. We thought he might be being an overly attached ex-boyfriend towards the now-slim Anders, but it turns out he was talking about FLASHPOINT.

You might have seen Thorin's take, or MonteCristo's about the unique approach that FLASHPOINT is touting, but if you want to hear from a guy that's not a) the creative director and b) haven't worked in CS:GO before, Semmler is your guy.

Money makes the world go round, and it makes your CSGO rounds a little bit easier, especially against ecozera. Semmler talks money and culture, but not the buying guns type - more the 'how the hell will this whole thing work' type.

If reading his words isn't enough for you, you really should watch the full video-interview where you can hear his dulcet French-ish tones. It's a bit longer than reading our newsletter, but the good thing about videos is you can watch them whilst playing deathmatch.

smash and burn
 

SMASHstralis take revenge

Complexity won't be winning a Major any time soon - hell, they won't be winning a Minor.

Heartbreak in the final round of a gruelling best of three means we won't be seeing everyone's favourite juggernaut at the Minor. They were dumped out by SMASH, a mix team of languages, cultures and nationalities - which finally proves that chaos is better than cohesion, as SMASH did what Astralis couldn't.

Complexity went from smashing ZywOo and Astralis 2-0 to losing to SMASH in the Minor Qualifiers. Yes, it was online instead of LAN, but we all know online is where the real stars come to play.

SMASH joined Copenhagen Flames in the Closed Qualifier after they dumped out Polish side Illuminar. Minor Qualifiers are basically AcilioN's second home at this point, and he helped an inexperienced Danish side through to the next round of qualifying.
