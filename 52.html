Two million ways to spell Tier 2
 

FLASHPOINT announce their league

Source: Flashpoint

Got a spare $2m?

Of course you do. Can we have some?

So, apparently, do C9, MiBR, MAD Lions and co. who have bought into FLASHPOINT, which is what Thorin and FACEIT's labour of love, previously known as 'B Site', is called.

Thorin spoke at length to GGIntel about the new league, dropping banger lines like '“I feel like we’re building an escape capsule for Counter-Strike and we’re hopefully going to escape the dumpster fire that is the other franchise leagues".

MonteCristo, who has more recently worked in Overwatch and League of Legends, has helped design the format, and also mentioned in an interview how much he wants to bring back trash talk to CSGO and annoy some sponsors along the way - which seems to our puny brains, counter-intuitive.

The format is kinda weird, so we recommend watching the video they provided if you're all that bothered. The short of it is that teams draft their own four-team groups, play each other, then do the same thing again and the teams with the most points go to the playoffs. Obviously.

10 of the 12 teams are partner teams who bought in, but two will be qualifier teams. Anyone can qualify - in fact, TLDR's own aizyesque tried. It... didn't go well. For the closed qualifiers, an impressive list of tier three teams was released, including steel's team Chaos.

Thorin has been boisterous on Twitter for the last few weeks, but he did have to apologise for one mistake.

Yes, Thorin. Apologised. We don't know either. Look, we know it's kinda cool that he was willing to say sorry and admit his mistake. That takes some balls, and we respect him for it.

But you wouldn't have to use those balls if you hadn't put them in so deep in the first place. Just, bro, take a breath before you go so hard next time.

Finally on this, ex-CR4ZY, now c0ntact Gaming, had their logo leaked. Reddit called - it wants its... thing back.

Another one
 

Mousesports makes it four out of five

You know like when your favourite band releases an album after four years, but it turns out that there's four concept tracks at the end and you'd heard three of the songs before? That bittersweet feeling is what electronic from Na`Vi must be feeling.

He got a career first MVP at ICE Challenge despite losing the Final convincingly, dropping 90 kills in four maps and trying to carry NaVi on his back. Even s1mple, who was surprisingly quiet all tournament long. Not even that surprising any more, though. 

His... career first? Yeah, apparently. 

We assume this is like how it took 3 years for anyone to pick up gla1ve and when Astralis finally did, they built a friggin' empire around the guy.

mousesports have now won four of the last five finals at big LAN events, after they smashed NaVi 3-1 in the final of the ICE Challenge. Weird, we thought FaZe were only choking because of karrigan?

Not only that, but they've been to five finals in a row, which is really making a case for them being the best team in the world right now. Keep it quiet, obviously. We don't want Astralis fanboys to be tweeting us.

Young'uns ropz and frozen put on a joint carry with 83 and 82 kills respectively. Speaking of joints, though, Dutchman chrisj had comfortably the play of the final. No, the play of the year. No, the play of the decade... (not even sorry about this one, it's THAT good).

The veteran pulled off an absurd 1v5 on Train that included a saucy little one tap in the 1v2. Filth.

Elsewhere, MAD Lions carried on their emergence as a top 10 team, taking down OG 2-0 and taking a map off of eventual winners mousesports. Second best team in Denmark? Sure. Top five in the world? Quite possibly.

OG on the other hand, are disappointing so far. None more so than valde, according to himself. The grass isn't always greener on the other side, especially when you're no longer overlooking a pitch full of the stuff.

qikert lost four out of six of the maps he played and ended with a 1.16 rating. Seems like 'playing with your friends' is so much better than playing with NaVi, right? You could be getting 1.16 ratings and getting let down by s1mple, like electronic, instead.

GODSENT might be interesting for the first time since... well, ever, with Maden looking super good. We're not sure where all these Serbian players keep coming from.
