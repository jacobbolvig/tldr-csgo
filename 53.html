k0nfigured to win
 

BLAST-ra-lose

Illustration by Roma Bib

Santa Claus got Astralis the best Christmas present - a big ol' slice of humble pie.

After gla1ve's prophetic tweet just before Christmas, we got to see that if Santa is real, he certainly has a sense of humour that we can appreciate.

For the rest of us, the present is getting to see a CS scene not dominated by one team - well, unless you count Complexity, who are now going to dominate the entire scene if this tournament is anything to go by. Nothing like a good overreaction.

dev1ce got absolutely dominated by the Bulgarian Bulld- uh, something else beginning with B. Poizon went crazy, and clearly dev1ce was scared. So, so scared, that he wouldn't even attempt a retake - and not in the way he normally shies away from a fight.

Not only that, but the Danes then got bodied by NaVi on Inferno. Despite having a 13-5 lead, Astralis didn't even make overtime, with a stunning electronic triple kill while stood in a molotov closing it out. Electronic fires are notoriously hard to put out.

We were getting bored of Astralis being good, so we're big fans of this. Astralis still have a chance to get into the BLAST Premier Spring Finals, but will have to qualify through the BLAST Showdown later this year.

electronic is numba one
 

Na`Vi finding Perfecto ending

Complexity hadn't lost a map after two games, as after 2-0'ing Astralis, the juggernaut rolled over ZywOo and co. ALEX doesn't think the juggernaut is unstoppable, as he mentioned in his post match interview that they were just running around headshotting, and Vitality weren't, and so long-term that wouldn't work.

Pardon our ignorance, ALEX, but if they keep headshotting and you don't, aren't they just better than you? Maybe his criticisms would hold more weight if their plan wasn't basically just 'pray to the shrine of a teenager' and hope for the best.

It must have been easy for coL against Vitality - BlameF reckons he knew the veto beforehand.

s1mple might have lost the war for #1, but this battle went his way. NaVi looked comfortable in outshooting Vitality on Inferno and Dust 2. In truth, neither s1mple nor ZywOo were the shining light - maybe 2020 is flamie's year for #1. We won't hold our breath.

ALEX seemed to think that Complexity just rocking up and headshotting off the server was just a honeymoon period, but if Vitality keep getting beasted by aggressive teams who keep fighting them, maybe that's a real issue.

At least RpK can still shoot straight.

Complexity finally found their match in s1mple, ironically enough. 2019's second best player decided he was gonna take over again, and absolutely smashed Complexity with a +21 K/D. Poetic.

k0nfig was the man who so far, looked like the one who could match him, but he had an off-game and that spelt doom for Complexity. They still got through though, unlike Astralis. Hahaha!

Uh oh not this early
 

Already a FLASHPOINT in qualifying

The third Minor Qualifier was dominated by Scandinavia, but probably not in the way you would imagine. KOVA, a Finnish team that isn't ENCE or HAVU, took out Endpoint, a UK team that isn't... uh, whatever team smooya is on, to qualify.

Norwegian team Apeks came back from the brink to reverse sweep Pompa despite the Poles having three series points on map two in regulation. Pompa had beaten out highest seed and fellow Polish team AGO, but losing to the second best Norwegian team won't exactly enthuse disillusioned Poles looking for a home since VP went Kazakh.

FLASHPOINT qualifiers went entirely differently - that is to say, DRAMA.

According to the players involved - an infamously unbiased source - the highest seeded team, forZe, were beaten fair and squarely by Yacine Laghmari and his team. But mysteriously, the tournament bracket was 'reset' and forZe were allowed back in, due to 'unforeseen circumstances'. Or unforZeen circumstances.

Yacine was mocked for being kicked from FPL by an admin, too. Although it's pretty immaterial to the story, it's an amateur display of professionalism.

Swedish player tabz was frustrated at potentially having to play til 2am if they were to qualify. He raises a good point, but don't worry fella, not much chance of that.

We can't wait to hear Thorin's justification for this one.
